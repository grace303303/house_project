Enviromental Info about
Superfunds

The Superfund program is designed to clean up sites contaminated with hazardous waste. Toxic substances at these sites could poison soil, water or air, causing human health risks like cancer, birth defects, nerve damage and liver damage.

Enviromental Cleanup Sites

Sites require cleanup may impact or have the potential to impact water quality, such as Leaking Underground Storage Tank (LUST) Sites. This map also includes records for various unregulated projects as well as permitted facilities.

Pipelines

You need to manually update address to access this map after redirecting. This map includes natural gas transmission pipelines and breakout tanks, whose release may impact environment nearby or cause accidents like fire or explosions. If a pipeline traverses your property, there might be rules about what you can and can't do in the pipeline area.

Earthquake zones

Earthquake could cause property damage, and people getting hurt or killed. Landslides and liquefaction are secondary effects caused by ground shaking, which are included in this map as well.
